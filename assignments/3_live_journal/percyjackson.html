Percy Jackson

"Percy Jackson Is Superior to Harry Potter"-Emily Que

As I walked through my elementary school library, I looked for new stories to begin reading. As I wandered throughout shelves of fairytales and Scholastic children stories I realized I was running out of time before my class would leave to head back to our teacher's classroom. I quickly grabbed a blue covered book from the shelf and checked it out hoping I could at least survive 10 minutes of reading time with this book I had grabbed.

Little did I know, this was my first step into a world I had never known before.

The next couple years were a blur. After recieving the rest of the Percy Jackson series as my 5th grade promotion present, I read through all the books as quickly as possible. I treated each book like it was my child, taking the utmost of care for each one.

As the series came to an end, I pondered on what my life would be after this series. How could I move on from such an important stage of my life. How could I say goodbye to all these characters that I've bonded with for years and years?

Then suddenly I realized I didn't need to, Rick Riordan had written a second series continuing on with the story from the first five books. Relief washed over me as I realize goodbye would be a little farther away than I had originally thought.

The Lost Hero, Son of Neptune, Mark of Athena, House of Hades, and Blood of Olympus all flew bly in a blur within the next couple years. The ups and downs of each book kept me on the edge of my seat for the next. I fell more and more in love with each character both old and new. These stories were a part of me and my life.

Sadly, all good things must come to an end, and I knew these characters wouldn't stay with me forever. The story of Percy Jackson has made an incredibly impact on my life and on who I am. I will forever be thankful of Rick Riordan and the day a random 5th grade teacher from Boston decided to write down his mythical stories that he made up for his ADHD diagnosed and Dyslexic son.

Thanks for all the memories, they will always live on within me and millions of others around the world.
